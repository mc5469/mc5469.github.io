Postscript

我的名字叫做梦瑶，这是一个很常见的中国女孩的名字。
我出生于2002年，彼时的中国处于改革开放后的新世纪中，蓝色的玻璃，外立面贴砖的大楼， 颇具苏联遗风的赫鲁晓夫楼一般的设计（参考汕头站），是我对于中国建筑最早期的记忆。 我无数次在深夜梦中回到这个场景，不锈钢的楼梯扶手，透过蓝色玻璃照进来的变了颜色的阳光，马赛克贴砖的地面，规整设计的大楼，深黄色的木质家具。 尽管今天的中国标志性的新建筑早就不长这样了，这些建于90年代00年代的“老建筑”，也正在被拆除，但这样的建筑依然是中国城市的底色。 对新世纪和未来的向往，房地产经济的腾飞。人们在城市里建起高大的地标，摩天大楼拔地而起。（目前中国已经出台了对于摩天大楼建设的限制法案）就像摩天大楼原理那样，拔地而起的摩天大楼是经济危机的前兆。 于是一切好像在某一年的某一天我看到我爸爸突然衰老的面孔一样，欣欣向荣的童话世界不复从前。

My name is Mengyao, a very common name for Chinese girls.
I was born in 2002, at a time when China was entering a new century post-reform and opening-up. My earliest memories of Chinese architecture include buildings with blue glass and tiled facades, reminiscent of the Khrushchev-era designs influenced by the Soviet Union (like the Shantou Station).
Countless times in my dreams at night, I return to this scene: stainless steel stair railings, sunlight colored through the blue glass, mosaic-tiled floors, systematically designed buildings, and dark yellow wooden furniture.
Even though China's iconic new buildings today no longer look like this, these "old buildings" constructed in the 90s and 00s are being demolished, yet they remain the underlying tone of Chinese cities.
The aspiration for the new century and the future, the soaring real estate economy. People build towering landmarks in cities, with skyscrapers rising from the ground. (China has now enacted restrictions on the construction of skyscrapers), just like the principle of skyscrapers, those that spring from the ground are a harbinger of economic crisis.
Thus, everything seems like the day I saw my father's suddenly aged face, the thriving fairy tale world is no longer exist.

Shantou Station

我从来不是一名真正的建筑师，我也不认为自己属于任何的头衔与称号。几个星期前有人问我:一开始你为什么选择这个专业学习，我说：哦！我在读高中的时候看到了梁思成的笔记， 他手绘的佛光寺。我觉得太不可思议了。我渴望和他成为一样的人。

I have never been a real architect, nor do I consider myself to belong to any title or designation. A few weeks ago, someone asked me why I chose to study this major in the first place. I said: Oh! In high school, I saw Liang Sicheng's notes, his hand-drawn Foguang Temple. I found it incredible. I longed to be like him.

Hand-drawn drawing by Liang Sicheng

这也是中国的建筑学科的开端。中国的古建筑有着非常强烈的魅力，匠人营造的法门，将一生寄托给乔木。尽管有很多的岁月的侵蚀，可是当你还能亲眼见到那些千年之前的幸存者的时候，那些残破的木结构，斑驳的颜色。 那是我的文化的底色和我的父辈们的前世今生，我没有办法不爱它们。

This is also the beginning of the architectural discipline in China. Ancient Chinese architecture has a strong allure, strongly connected with wooden materials. Despite the erosion of thousands years, but you can still see those survivors from thousands years ago, the broken wooden structures, the mottled colors.
This is my people, this is my culture, this is the past of the land I love.

Mengyao And Honghao in Wudang Mountain

1949年，第二次世界大战废墟里建立起的新国家。想要和过去决裂，想要创造新的世界。历史的巨变和文化思潮凝聚在一栋栋的建筑里，能看见野心，能看见理想主义，能看见荒谬，直到今天。从行业巅峰跌下来的建筑学， 从被称为是新世纪的新希望的千禧一代到开始用一场又一场的考试，企图摄入“范进中举”美梦的精神鸦片安慰自己的青年人。越来越多庞大先锋的异形建筑在中国的土地上落地生根，年轻的建筑师们抱怨设计院吃掉他们的灵魂。

In 1949, a new country was established from the ruins of World War II. There was a desire to break with the past and create a new world. The great changes in history and cultural trends are condensed in each building, where you can see ambition, idealism, and absurdity, even to this day.From the pinnacle of the architectural industry, from the millennials called the new hope of the new century to the youth who try to comfort themselves with the opium named "Fanjin Zhong Ju" in one exam after another.More and more massive avant-garde exotic buildings take root in the land of China, with young architects complaining that architectural companies are devouring their souls.

我想要知道，打开新中国大门的钥匙到底挂在谁的腰间？打开建筑学的明天的钥匙到底挂在谁的腰间？

I can help to wonder, who holds the key to opening the doors of new China? Who holds the key to the future of architecture?
