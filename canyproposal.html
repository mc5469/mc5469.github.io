Where is Capernaum?

Mengyao(cany) Chen

Hi I'm Trying to do something new about Chinatown: Flushing, NYC

In my past life, I have hardly ever left mainland China. I was born there, grew up there, and received my education there. I love my people and cherish the land with its rich culture. So, when I look back at the changes that have occurred over the past forty years, from 1980 to the present, it is a deeply painful process for me. I can see the people on this land struggling valiantly, yet feeling powerless against the currents of time. I can see the enthusiasm and hope that once soared being turned into numbness and ashes. Placing myself in the larger context of the era, I feel like I am gazing into an abyss.

Patriarchy, feudalism, authoritarianism, formalism, the absence of democracy... After undergoing nearly a decade of strict political scrutiny, I sadly find that I have adopted a self-censoring style of writing. Reflecting on history in an objective manner and contemplating them before everything dissipates, even if it's accurate, will be labeled incorrectly (this is also what I've learned from history). Even though it requires great courage to look back at these pains, peel back that thin layer of scab, and confront the raw wounds, I feel it is worth it. It's something that must be done.

So I'm going to talk about Flushing, about politics, about enclave, about counties in China.

所以当我回望从1980年到如今的四十年之间的变迁的时候，对我来说是一件非常痛苦的过程， 我能看到这片土地上的人们的奋力挣扎而又被时代洪流裹挟的无能为力， 我能看到那些被扬起的热情与希望变成麻木和灰烬。

把自己置于时代的大背景时， 我感觉我在凝视深渊。

父权制，封建观念，专制，形式主义，不存在的民主…… 还有经历了接近十年严格的政治审查后，我发现自己已经可悲的养成了自我阉割的写作方式。
以一种很客观的方式回望历史，在一切都烟消云散之前对他们进行思考，哪怕是正确的 ，也会被打上错误的标签（这也是我从历史里学到的）。
即使需要很大的勇气回望这些苦痛， 揭开那一层薄薄痂，直面鲜血淋漓的血肉。但我觉得是值得的，这是必须要做的事情。
